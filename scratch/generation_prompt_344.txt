1. Brand Voice & Pacing (Sound Human):
   - Write from the collective perspective of the company or team using first-person plural pronouns (we, our, us) to convey collective expertise, keeping the tone collaborative, human, and authoritative. Avoid singular personal pronouns (I, my, me) that claim individual personal experience.
   - Write from operational experience rather than general explanation. The article should prioritize usefulness over theoretical completeness. Leave simple, obvious concepts unexplained if the target audience (practitioners) would already know them.
   - Address actual tradeoffs, operational constraints, decisions, and outcomes. Focus on real-world workflow bottlenecks, implementation friction, or common recurring mistakes.
   - Use clear, plain, conversational B2B English. Vary paragraph and sentence lengths dramatically—mix very short, punchy sentences with longer ones. Use occasional single-sentence paragraphs.

2. AEO (Answer Engine Optimization) Structure:
   - Direct Answer Summaries: At the very beginning of key sections, provide a clear, direct, 1-to-2 sentence answer to the section's core query. This makes the content easily extractable for AI search summaries (Perplexity, SGE) and highly skimmable for humans.
   - Query-Based & Editorial Headings: Avoid educational or predictable headings like "Introduction", "Conclusion", "What is X", "Benefits of X", "Key Features", "Final Thoughts". Instead, write editorial headings that reflect choices, operational realities, implementation lessons, or tradeoffs (e.g. "The Hidden Costs of...", "Tradeoffs in...", "Where Teams Repeatedly Stumble"). Some headings should be phrased as natural user search queries.
   - Conditional Q&A / FAQ Section: Add a conversational Q&A / FAQ section at the end of the post *only* when it naturally supports search intent and fills information gaps. Do not force-fit FAQs if the topic does not warrant it.
   - Allow sections to end abruptly when the point has been made. Do not force neat summaries or lessons at the end of every section.
